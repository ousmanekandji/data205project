# Data 205 Capstone Report

## Food Inspections and Closed Establishments

---

## 1. Inspection Outcome Distribution

**Inspection Results ~ Count of Records**

* Pass inspections dominate the dataset
* Fail and Closed outcomes represent a much smaller portion

**Key Insight:**
Most food establishments meet basic compliance standards. However, the presence of failures and closures indicates that food safety risks still exist across inspections.

---

## 2. Closure Share

**Closure Share = Closed Inspection Rows / Total Inspection Rows**

* Closures represent a very small percentage of all inspection activity

**Key Insight:**
Closures are rare events, but they represent the most severe enforcement outcome. Even though uncommon, they are critical indicators of major food safety violations.

---

## 3. Violations Driving Closures

**Out-of-Compliance Violations ~ Frequency in Closure Data**

Top recurring violation categories:

* Temperature control (hot/cold holding, cooling)
* Food contamination issues
* Hygiene and handwashing violations
* Pest presence (rodents and insects)

**Key Insight:**
Closures are not random events. They are consistently driven by a small group of high-risk violations.

**Interpretation:**

* Temperature and sanitation failures are the strongest indicators of closure
* Minor violations do not typically lead to closures
* Severe violations accumulate and trigger enforcement

---

## 4. Closure Trend Over Time

**Closures ~ Monthly Count**

* Closure events occur consistently across months
* No clear downward trend observed

**Key Insight:**
Food safety risks persist over time rather than declining.

**Interpretation:**

* Enforcement remains necessary
* Risk conditions are ongoing, not temporary

---

## 5. Geographic Distribution of Closures

**Closures ~ City-Level Counts**

* Some cities show higher closure counts
* Likely influenced by:

  * Higher population
  * More food establishments
  * Increased inspection frequency

**Key Insight:**
Closure counts vary geographically, but raw counts must be interpreted carefully.

**Interpretation:**
Higher counts do not necessarily mean worse safety conditions—they may reflect higher inspection volume.

---

## 6. Overall Findings

* Most inspections result in compliance
* Closures are rare but consistently present
* A small number of violations drive most closures
* Temperature control, sanitation, and pest issues are key risk factors

---

## 7. Limitations

* Data is recorded at the violation level, not fully aggregated per inspection
* Closure counts are not normalized by total inspections per city
* Analysis is descriptive and does not establish causality

---

## 8. Conclusion

Food establishment closures are uncommon but predictable events driven by repeated critical violations. Identifying these patterns can help target enforcement efforts and improve public health outcomes.
